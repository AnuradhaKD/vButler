## User Story

**As a** hotel guest,
**I want** to browse spa treatment cards with key details and expand any treatment to read full information before booking,
**So that** I can choose the treatment most suited to my preferences and understand what is included before committing to a booking.

---

## Background / Context

Spa treatment selection is a considered decision for guests — they want to understand duration, what the treatment entails, inclusions (e.g., welcome drink, use of facilities), and the cancellation policy before booking. Presenting this information in a card with an expandable detail view keeps the Wellness Hub scannable while giving guests all the information they need to proceed confidently to the booking flow.

---

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Spa treatment cards are displayed within the "Spa Treatments" section of the Wellness Hub (US-08.1)
- [ ] Each card displays: treatment name, duration as a chip (e.g., "60 min"), price in LKR formatted with comma separator, and a short description (truncated to 2 lines)
- [ ] Tapping a treatment card expands it inline OR opens a bottom drawer (bottom sheet), showing full details
- [ ] Full detail view contains: full description, inclusions list (bullet points), cancellation policy text, and a "Book Now" button
- [ ] Inclusions are sourced from `treatment.inclusions[]` array (e.g., ["Welcome herbal tea", "Use of steam room", "Post-treatment relaxation lounge access"])
- [ ] Cancellation policy text is sourced from `treatment.cancellationPolicy` string in JSON; if absent, display "Please contact the spa for cancellation terms"
- [ ] "Book Now" button in the detail view navigates to the Wellness Booking Flow (US-08.4) with the `treatmentId` pre-populated
- [ ] Only one treatment can be expanded/open at a time; tapping a second treatment closes the first
- [ ] Closing the expanded view (via a close button or tapping outside) returns to the card list without navigation
- [ ] All treatment data is sourced from `property.json → wellness.treatments[]`
- [ ] Screen renders correctly in dark mode

## UI / UX Notes

- Card (collapsed): white background (dark: surface-card), 12 px border radius, 16 px padding, left accent border 4 px brand primary
- Card layout: treatment name (16 px semi-bold) top-left; duration chip top-right (brand accent bg, white text, 12 px, rounded); short description below in 13 px muted; price (LKR X,XXX) bottom-left in brand primary 15 px bold; "View Details" chevron bottom-right
- Duration chip: "60 min", "90 min", "120 min" — sourced from `treatment.durationMinutes` formatted as `N min`
- Expanded / bottom drawer: slides up from bottom on mobile (80% screen height, drag handle at top); smooth CSS transition 300 ms
- Full description: 14 px Inter, normal weight, full text without truncation
- Inclusions: bulleted list with a checkmark icon (brand primary colour) per item, 13 px
- Cancellation policy: amber info card (same as US-07.3), 13 px italic
- "Book Now" button: brand primary filled, full-width, 48 px, pinned to bottom of drawer
- Close button: "×" icon top-right of drawer, 44 px tap target

---

## Technical Notes

- Treatment cards rendered within Wellness Hub Alpine.js component; expansion state managed as `activeTreatmentId` (null if none open)
- Bottom drawer: Alpine `x-show="activeTreatmentId === treatment.id"` on a fixed-position overlay div; `@click.outside="activeTreatmentId = null"` for dismiss
- Price formatting: `formatLKR(treatment.price)` utility
- Duration formatting: `treatment.durationMinutes + " min"`
- "Book Now" navigation: `router.navigate('#/wellness/book?id=' + treatment.id)`
- Inclusions rendered with `x-for` over `treatment.inclusions[]`; if array absent, section hidden
- On expand, scroll treatment card into view using `$el.scrollIntoView({ behavior: 'smooth', block: 'nearest' })`

## Test Cases

### Happy Path

1. Guest views Wellness Hub; Spa Treatments section shows 4 cards: "Ayurvedic Abhyanga", "Hot Stone Massage", "Deep Tissue Therapy", "Couples Retreat"
2. Each card shows name, duration chip ("60 min", "90 min"), price (LKR 8,500 – LKR 22,000), and 2-line description
3. Guest taps "Ayurvedic Abhyanga" → bottom drawer slides up with full description, 3 inclusions (checkmarks), amber cancellation policy card
4. Guest taps "Book Now" → navigates to US-08.4 with treatmentId pre-populated
5. Guest closes drawer (taps ×) → returns to card list; "Ayurvedic Abhyanga" card collapsed

### Edge Cases

- **Treatment with no inclusions**: Inclusions section hidden; no bullet list
- **Cancellation policy absent**: "Please contact the spa for cancellation terms" shown instead
- **Two cards tapped in quick succession**: First drawer closes before second opens; no overlapping drawers
- **Treatment description under 80 chars**: No truncation; full text shown on card
- **Price = 0**: Card shows "Complimentary" instead of "LKR 0"
- **Dark mode**: Card borders, chips, drawer background, inclusion checkmarks all verified
