## User Story

**As a** guest,
**I want** to view, edit, and cancel my scheduled wake-up calls,
**So that** I can adjust my schedule without contacting the front desk.

---

## Background / Context

Guests may change their plans during their stay — a tour may be cancelled, a late night may push back a desired wake-up time, or a recurring call may no longer be needed. The management view gives guests full self-service control over their scheduled calls, removing friction and reducing front desk call volume for Browns Hotels & Resorts staff. All state changes are reflected in localStorage immediately.

---

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] All active (future, not yet triggered) wake-up calls for the current reservation are displayed at the top of the `#/wakeup-call` screen, above the "Schedule New Wake-Up Call" section, in chronological order (earliest first)
- [ ] Each wake-up call card displays: an alarm/bell icon, the scheduled date (formatted as "Weekday, D MMM" e.g. "Wednesday, 6 Apr"), the scheduled time (HH:MM AM/PM), a "Recurring" badge if `isRecurring: true`, and **Edit** and **Cancel** action buttons
- [ ] Tapping **Cancel** presents an inline confirmation prompt on the card ("Are you sure you want to cancel this wake-up call?") with "Yes, Cancel" and "Keep" buttons; confirming removes the entry from `vb:wakeupCalls:{resvId}[]` and appends a cancellation entry to `vb:requests[]` with `status: "cancelled"` and `cancelledAt` timestamp
- [ ] The Cancel button is disabled (greyed out, non-interactive) when the scheduled wake-up call is within 30 minutes of the current time; a tooltip or inline message reads "Cannot cancel within 30 minutes of scheduled time"
- [ ] Tapping **Edit** navigates the user to the scheduling form (the "Schedule New" section below, or scrolls to it) pre-filled with the existing call's time and date; on saving, the existing localStorage entry is replaced (not duplicated) and `vb:requests[]` is updated with `status: "modified"` and `modifiedAt` timestamp
- [ ] Multiple wake-up calls scheduled across different dates and times are each displayed as individual cards in chronological order
- [ ] Wake-up calls where `dayjs(call.scheduledAt).isBefore(dayjs())` evaluate to true are automatically filtered out of the active list and not rendered; they are considered past/archived
- [ ] If no active wake-up calls exist, the active calls section is hidden entirely (no empty state shown in that region); the "Schedule New Wake-Up Call" section is shown as the primary content
- [ ] If a recurring set of calls exists, each individual night is shown as a separate card (each with its own Cancel/Edit capability); the "Recurring" badge appears on each

## UI / UX Notes

- Section heading above the active calls list: "Your Scheduled Calls" with a small alarm icon, displayed only when at least one active call exists
- Call cards use a white (light mode) / dark surface (dark mode) card with subtle border and shadow
- Alarm icon on each card: Material Icons `alarm` or equivalent, in brand accent colour
- "Recurring" badge: small pill, muted brand colour, text "Recurring" in 11px uppercase
- Edit and Cancel buttons: text buttons (not full-width), right-aligned on the card; Cancel in a warning/destructive text colour (amber or red-muted)
- Inline confirmation on Cancel replaces the card action buttons area with the prompt; no modal/overlay to keep the experience lightweight
- Disabled Cancel button: 40% opacity, `cursor: not-allowed`; the tooltip appears on hover (desktop) or after long-press (mobile)
- Chronological ordering of cards is enforced via Alpine.js computed sort: `calls.sort((a,b) => dayjs(a.scheduledAt).diff(dayjs(b.scheduledAt)))`
- The divider between "Your Scheduled Calls" and "Schedule New Wake-Up Call" is a branded horizontal rule with a label ("Schedule Another Wake-Up Call")
- Mobile: cards are full-width; tablet+: max-width 480px centred

## Technical Notes

- **Day.js** for all date comparisons:
  - Past filtering: `calls.filter(c => dayjs(c.scheduledAt).isAfter(dayjs()))`
  - 30-minute cancellation constraint: `dayjs(call.scheduledAt).diff(dayjs(), 'minute') < 30`
  - Re-evaluated on component mount and every 60 seconds via `setInterval` to auto-archive calls that pass their scheduled time while the screen is open
- **localStorage operations**:
  - Read: `JSON.parse(localStorage.getItem('vb:wakeupCalls:' + resvId)) || []`
  - Cancel: filter out the entry by `id`, `setItem` updated array; append to `vb:requests[]` with `{ ...originalEntry, status: 'cancelled', cancelledAt: dayjs().toISOString() }`
  - Edit (pre-fill): pass the existing call object to the scheduling form's Alpine.js component via a shared store (`Alpine.store('editingCall', callObj)`)
  - Edit (save): replace entry by matching `id` in the array
- **Alpine.js** component (`x-data="wakeupManager()"`) manages:
  - `activeCalls` — filtered and sorted reactive array
  - `confirmingCancelId` — tracks which card is in confirmation state (only one at a time)
  - `init()` — loads from localStorage, starts 60-second interval for auto-archiving
  - `cancelCall(id)` — performs removal and logs to requests queue
  - `editCall(call)` — sets `Alpine.store('editingCall')` and scrolls to form
- When editing and saving, ensure the form's `submitCall()` checks `Alpine.store('editingCall')` to determine update vs. create path

## Test Cases

### Happy Path
1. Guest has 2 active wake-up calls: 06:30 AM tomorrow and 07:00 AM day after tomorrow
2. Navigate to `#/wakeup-call`
3. "Your Scheduled Calls" section appears above the form with 2 cards in chronological order
4. Both cards show correct time, date, alarm icon; neither has "Recurring" badge
5. Guest taps "Cancel" on the 06:30 AM card
6. Inline confirmation prompt appears; guest taps "Yes, Cancel"
7. Card disappears from the list; `vb:wakeupCalls:{resvId}` now has 1 entry; `vb:requests[]` has cancellation entry
8. Only the 07:00 AM card remains
9. Guest taps "Edit" on the 07:00 AM card
10. Form scrolls into view pre-filled with 07:00 AM and day after tomorrow's date
11. Guest changes time to 07:30 AM and taps "Schedule Wake-Up Call"
12. Card updates to 07:30 AM; no duplicate entry in localStorage

### Edge Cases
- **All calls are past**: `wakeupCalls` array has entries all before `dayjs()` → "Your Scheduled Calls" section is hidden; only the form is shown
- **Cancel within 30 minutes**: Call scheduled for 15 minutes from now → Cancel button is disabled, tooltip shown; app re-checks every 60 seconds
- **Edit then cancel without saving**: Guest taps Edit, scrolls to form, then taps Cancel on a different card → confirmation prompt appears; pre-filled form remains but is not submitted
- **Recurring set — cancel one**: Guest cancels one night from a recurring set → only that specific card is removed; others remain with "Recurring" badge
- **Auto-archive during active session**: A call's scheduled time passes while the screen is open → after the 60-second interval tick, the card disappears without manual refresh
- **Empty list after last cancellation**: Guest cancels the only active call → "Your Scheduled Calls" section heading and card area disappear; form section becomes the primary view
- **localStorage read failure**: Catch JSON parse errors gracefully; render empty active list with no console errors surfaced to UI
